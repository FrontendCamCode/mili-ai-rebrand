MRO: How are we doing and where are we going? — Part 1

Before you can improve your MRO supply chain, you need a baseline. Tim McLain introduces the first two of five KPIs that roll up to overall Supply Chain Effectiveness: Demand Signal Quality and Catalogued Material Spend.

Tim McLain November 4, 2024 MROKPIssupply chaindemand signal

Before implementing changes to MRO supply chain processes, organizations should establish baseline performance metrics. This piece focuses on measuring performance across Warehouse Operations, Inventory Management, Transactional Purchasing, and Procurement functions.

Five Key Performance Indicators

Five KPIs roll up to a single “Supply Chain Effectiveness” metric. This first installment addresses two:

  1. Demand Signal Quality %
  2. Catalogued Material Spend %

Demand Signal Quality %

Purpose: The demand signal triggers supply chain action. High-quality signals enable planned responses (efficient); poor signals force reactive responses (inefficient).

Definition: A signal is considered “planned” when the reservation release date exceeds the requirement date by more than seven days. This window allows the supply chain to organize picking, purchasing, receiving, packing, delivery, and issuing.

Common Warehouse Disruptions from unplanned visits:

  • Staff wanting to verify work order materials
  • Requests for materials not in work orders
  • Asking to temporarily borrow parts
  • Inquiring about ordered materials

Scheduled warehouse closures during business hours minimize unplanned disruptions. Staff can still enter for emergency breakdowns, but the restriction forces better planning overall.

Formula:

Released Reservation Date - Reservation Requirement Date > 7 days = HIT (< 7 days = MISS)

Results are reported monthly as a percentage of hits against total reservations issued. Free text purchases are excluded from this KPI.

Catalogued Material Spend %

The Free Text Problem: Free text spending represents uncontrolled, non-catalogued material purchases — “the evil twin of catalogue material spend.”

Ideal State: All spare parts should be catalogued with:

  • Equipment identification
  • BOM (Bill of Materials) inclusion
  • Sourcing data (info records and source lists in SAP)

Real-World Example: At a large Australian mining operation, a reduction initiative involved:

  • Rejecting free text requisitions when material numbers existed
  • Expanding cataloging resources to process requests within 24 hours
  • Reducing free text spend from 65% to under 8% in nine months
  • Doubling stock turns and reducing working capital by $30 million AUD

A key insight: “It is easier using material numbers instead of free texting materials.”

Formula:

Value of Catalogued Material Spend / Value of Total Material Spend

Target: increase catalogued material percentage toward 100%. Run reports by both value and volume.

“What we do not measure, we cannot understand. What we do not understand, we cannot improve.”
