MRO Purchasing Automation? Sorry, I'm too busy and comfortable with doing it manually.

Few businesses know what it actually costs them to generate a purchase order. Tim McLain breaks down the six building blocks of PO cost and the case for the Perfect PO.

Tim McLain July 11, 2024 purchasingautomationMROsupply chain

Most organizations underestimate the hidden costs of manual purchasing and miss significant optimization opportunities. “Few businesses have any idea how much it actually costs them to generate a PO.” This knowledge gap prevents companies from recognizing automation as a cost-reduction strategy.

Six Building Blocks for Calculating Purchasing Cost

  1. Requisition Creation — Manual processes are costlier than MRP-generated requisitions
  2. Requisition Release — Multiple approval levels increase expenses
  3. Req to PO Conversion — Manual conversions with poor data quality require costly revisions
  4. PO Expediting — Tracking time spent on follow-ups per line item
  5. Goods Receipting — Average processing time per line item
  6. Invoice Processing — Manual versus automated payment methods

The Perfect PO Concept

A Perfect PO is “automatically generated and transmitted to the Supplier where goods are delivered, receipted into stock, and issued per delivery date with zero changes,” then automatically processed and paid.

This ideal scenario requires only three human touchpoints: the demand signal, goods receipt, and goods issue.

Benefits of Automation

Rather than hiring staff focused on converting requisitions, organizations should employ purchasing data administrators who collect and manage information that drives automatic PO creation. This proactive approach captures more value than reactive purchasing.
