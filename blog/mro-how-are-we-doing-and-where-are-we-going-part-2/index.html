MRO: How are we doing and where are we going? — Part 2

The five MRO supply chain KPIs are interconnected — poor demand signals breed free text spending, which undermines stock accuracy, which kills automation. Tim McLain maps the spiderweb and introduces Stock Turns.

Tim McLain December 9, 2024 MROKPIssupply chainstock turns

The five key performance indicators for MRO supply chain effectiveness function interconnectedly rather than as isolated measures. Avoid overly rigid measurement systems that stifle creativity.

The Three Pillars Framework

The five KPIs align with three foundational pillars of the MRO supply chain, all supported by master data. Previously covered: Demand Quality % and Catalogued Material Spend %, which provide insight into information quality flowing through the system.

Spiderweb of Connections

The KPIs interdependently support or hinder supply chain effectiveness:

  • Demand Signal Quality: Poor demand signals create either material shortages or excess inventory; strong signals reduce working capital by limiting stocking to essential breakdowns
  • Free Text Purchasing: Weak demand signals correlate with higher manual purchasing, reducing negotiated spend and stock turnover
  • Stock Accuracy: Poor accuracy breeds warehouse trust issues and encourages workarounds; strong accuracy reduces operational risk and unplanned spending
  • Purchasing Automation: Limited automation increases lead times and stock levels; robust automation supports cost reduction and better master data

“Poor Stock Turns is usually the result of a large volume of SLOBEX stock that never gets reviewed.”

Stock Turns %

Converting traditional stock turnover metrics into percentages enables comprehensive KPI rollup into overall Supply Chain Effectiveness %.

ABC Analysis Categories:

  • Fast movers: turns exceeding 0.85
  • Intermediate: turns between 0.45–0.85
  • SLOB (Slow and Obsolete): turns below 0.45

Every organization maintains some slow-moving inventory. Formal annual reviews of slow-moving materials are essential, and retained SLOB items should be documented in BOMs with identified suppliers in ERP systems.
