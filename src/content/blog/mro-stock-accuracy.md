## Calling all CONTROL Freaks!

Two contrasting scenarios define the stock accuracy divide:

**When stocks are accurate:** Trust + Less noise + Less unplanned work + Lower costs

**When stocks are inaccurate:** Over ordering + Squirrel stores + Expediting + Constant noise

Planners and schedulers depend on accurate ERP inventory data. Without it, they resort to unauthorized purchasing, excess material reserves, and creating informal storage locations — perpetuating operational inefficiency.

*"Stocktakes need to be viewed as a gap analysis that identifies the level of control that you have over your stock."*

## The Core Problem

Many warehouses simply adjust numbers to match physical counts without investigating root causes. This practice masks underlying process failures and erodes stakeholder confidence.

## Moving to a Cycle Counting Program

The recommended approach uses ABC classification:

- **A Class:** Fast-moving, critical materials — counted monthly
- **B Class:** Moderate-moving materials — counted quarterly
- **C Class:** Slow-moving materials — counted twice annually
- **D Class:** Non-critical, rarely used materials — counted annually

## Measurement

Stock accuracy should be calculated by both quantity and value, with results shared across departments for continuous improvement discussion.
