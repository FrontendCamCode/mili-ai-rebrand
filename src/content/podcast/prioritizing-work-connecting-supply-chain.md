Work prioritisation and supply chain planning are typically treated as separate functions. In practice, they're deeply connected: the sequence in which maintenance work is executed should be informed by material availability, and material procurement priorities should be driven by work schedules.

Tim and Justin explore what it looks like when these two functions operate in genuine coordination — and what happens when they don't.

**Key topics:**
- Why maintenance and procurement need to operate from the same planning horizon
- How material availability should influence work scheduling
- The role of the weekly coordination meeting — when it works and when it doesn't
- Technology's role in connecting the two disciplines
