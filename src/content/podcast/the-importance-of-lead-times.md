Lead time is one of the most important inputs in any MRO stocking model. It's also one of the most commonly wrong. Quoted lead times from suppliers are aspirational; measured lead times from purchase order history are real. The gap between the two is where safety stock failures hide.

Tim and Justin make the case for measuring, maintaining, and actively using accurate lead time data — and explain the practical mechanics of how to do it.

**Key topics:**
- Why quoted and actual lead times diverge
- How to calculate lead times from PO history
- The relationship between lead time accuracy and safety stock
- Lead time as a supplier performance metric
