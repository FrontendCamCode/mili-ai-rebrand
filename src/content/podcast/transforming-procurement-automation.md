Procurement automation is a well-worn topic. The practical question for MRO teams isn't whether to automate — it's which parts of the procurement process benefit from automation, and which parts require human judgment that technology can't replicate.

Tim and Justin separate the automation hype from the practical reality, drawing on real implementations rather than vendor presentations.

**Key topics:**
- Routine replenishment automation — the conditions required
- Where the exception layer resists automation
- Supplier integration and EDI in an MRO context
- The data quality prerequisite that most automation projects underestimate
