Cataloguing materials — creating complete, accurate, standardised records in the material master — is the least exciting part of MRO management. It's also the most foundational. Almost every downstream problem in purchasing, inventory, and maintenance planning can be traced back to a material cataloguing failure.

This episode makes the case for cataloguing as a strategic investment, not an administrative overhead, and covers the principles that separate good cataloguing practice from bad.

**Key topics:**
- What a properly catalogued material record contains
- The downstream consequences of poor cataloguing
- Noun/modifier convention and why it matters for search
- How cataloguing investment pays back through better purchasing and planning
