Material Requirements Planning (MRP) was designed for manufacturing environments. Applying it to maintenance materials — with their irregular demand, long and uncertain lead times, and criticality-driven stocking requirements — requires modification and care.

This short episode covers the fundamentals of MRP in an MRO context: where it adds value, where it creates noise, and what configuration choices make the biggest difference.

**Key topics:**
- MRP basics for maintenance practitioners
- Why standard MRP settings produce poor results for MRO
- The configuration choices that improve MRP output for maintenance materials
- When to use MRP and when to use simpler reorder point logic
