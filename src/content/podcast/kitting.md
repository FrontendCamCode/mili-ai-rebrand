Kitting — the process of assembling and staging all required materials before a maintenance job starts — is one of the highest-leverage activities in planned maintenance. A well-kitted job starts on time, runs without interruption, and produces accurate consumption records. An unkitted job waits for parts.

Tim and Justin break down the kitting process, the prerequisites that make it work, and the common failure modes that prevent operations from realising its benefits.

**Key topics:**
- What kitting requires from BOMs and material masters
- The logistics of physical kitting and staging
- How kitting affects maintenance schedule compliance
- Common failure modes — and how to address them
