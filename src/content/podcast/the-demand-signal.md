MRO demand is different from direct material demand. It's lumpy, irregular, and often driven by failures that can't be predicted with certainty. Understanding the nature of the demand signal — where it comes from, how it behaves, and how it should inform inventory decisions — is fundamental to getting stocking right.

Tim and Justin cover the different types of MRO demand (planned maintenance, corrective work, project requirements, and genuine emergencies), and how each should be treated differently in stocking models.

**Key topics:**
- The four types of MRO demand and their characteristics
- Why averaging demand hides the variability that matters
- How planned maintenance schedules should drive forward-looking demand
- The difference between consumption-based and requirements-based stocking approaches
