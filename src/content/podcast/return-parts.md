In this episode Tim and Justin cover the often-overlooked practice of returning unused parts to the storeroom. It sounds straightforward. In practice, parts end up in technician tool kits, at job sites, or simply lost — creating inventory inaccuracy and driving unnecessary re-purchasing.

The conversation covers the process controls that make returns systematic, the system transactions that keep inventory records accurate, and why the discipline of returns is as important as the discipline of issues.

**Key topics:**
- Why parts don't make it back to stores
- The system transaction side of returns processing
- Condition assessment at return
- How poor returns discipline inflates inventory and purchasing spend
